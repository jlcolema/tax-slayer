Frequently Asked Questions

How does refundNOW work?

The loan amount ($500 or $1,000) will be subtracted from the total tax refund amount issued by the IRS. In other words, the loan amount will reduce any tax refund amount you get later.

Remember, the IRS must accept your tax return before a refund advance can be processed. Once the IRS accepts your tax return, it generally takes at least 48 hours to get the refund advance.

refundNOW is provided by River City Bank, Member FDIC.

How soon can I apply for refundNOW?

Once you’ve received the tax documents necessary to e-file your tax return—and after you’ve e-filed with TaxSlayer using Classic or Premium –you can apply for refundNOW. Funds are limited so don’t wait to apply!

Why do I have to wait 48 hours to get money?

The IRS must accept your tax return before the tax refund advance is processed. Processing generally takes at least 48 hours.

How do I get the money once I’m approved for refundNOW?

You have two options: (1) Money is deposited into your bank account via direct deposit. OR (2) Money is loaded onto an American Express Serve® Prepaid Card.

Is refundNOW a loan?

Yes, refundNOW is an optional non-recourse loan with no interest. refundNOW is available at no additional cost. The loan amount ($500 or $1000) will be subtracted from any tax refund amount issued by the IRS.

What are the eligibility requirements for refundNOW?

To be eligible for refundNOW, you must have a W-2 and a current government-issued photo ID. Your eligibility also depends on the tax refund amount, verification of your identity, other criteria, and underwriting standards. Other terms and conditions may apply. refundNOW is not available in Illinois.

How much money can I get?

The amount of the tax refund advance depends on the tax refund amount but will not be more than $1,000.

How is refundNOW different from my tax refund?

refundNOW is not the actual tax refund. refundNOW is an optional non-recourse loan. You can file a tax return electronically without applying for a refund transfer or refundNOW.

Is there a fee for refundNOW?

There are no upfront fees for refundNOW, but tax preparation fees will apply, such as the purchase of the TaxSlayer Classic or Premium package. Other fees, such as the refund transfer service, may apply and will be subtracted from the tax refund. Note that the refund transfer service, provided by River City Bank, Member FDIC, is not required to apply for refundNOW.

Form 1040EZ is the simplest form to fill out.
You may use Form 1040EZ if you meet all the following conditions:

  • Your filing status is single or married filing jointly
  • You claim no dependents
  • You, and your spouse if filing a joint return, were under age 65 on January 1, 2014, and not blind at the end of 2013
  • You have only wages, salaries, tips, taxable scholarship and fellowship grants, unemployment compensation, or Alaska Permanent Fund dividends, and your taxable interest was not over $1,500
  • Your taxable income is less than $100,000
  • Your earned tips, if any, are included in boxes 5 and 7 of your Form W-2
  • You are not a debtor in a Chapter 11 bankruptcy case filed after October 16, 2005
  • You do not claim any adjustments to income, such as a deduction for IRA contributions, a student loan interest deduction, an educator expenses deduction, or a tuition and fees deduction
  • You do not claim any credits other than the earned income credit

If you file Form 1040EZ, you cannot itemize deductions or claim any adjustments to income or tax credits (other than the earned income credit).
